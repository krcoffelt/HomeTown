About HomeTown

We're a Kansas City marketing agency on a mission to help local businesses thrive in the digital age.

Hey, I'm Kyle Coffelt

I'm a Kansas City local with a passion for helping small businesses grow. My journey took an unexpected turn when I met my wife in Sydney, Australia. After our adventure down under, we decided to make KC our home, and I've never looked back.

Since returning home, I've felt a strong calling to empower local small businesses. I understand that most business owners are stretched thin – they don't have the time, bandwidth, or budget for complex marketing strategies. That's where I come in.

My mission is simple: bridge the gap between where your business is now and where it could be. I handle the marketing heavy lifting so you can focus on what matters most – running your business and serving your customers.

Why I Do What I Do

Every day, I see incredible local businesses – from family-owned cafes in Westport to boutique shops in the Crossroads – struggling to get noticed in an increasingly digital world. These are the businesses that make Kansas City special, and they deserve to thrive.

I believe in affordable, effective marketing that actually moves the needle. No jargon, no inflated promises – just genuine help from someone who understands both the local market and the challenges of running a small business.

Why HomeTown?

After my time in Sydney, I came back to Kansas City with fresh eyes. I saw a city full of passionate entrepreneurs and small business owners who pour their hearts into what they do. But I also saw how many were struggling with marketing – not because they didn't care, but because they were already wearing too many hats.

That's when HomeTown was born. Not as another expensive agency with complicated packages, but as a genuine partner for local businesses. I keep things simple, affordable, and focused on what actually works.

Every day, I get to help businesses like yours reach more customers, build stronger brands, and achieve the growth they deserve. It's not just about marketing metrics – it's about making Kansas City's local economy stronger, one business at a time.
